Foods

Taiwanese cuisine is a blend of various regional Chinese cuisines, along with influences from Japanese and indigenous Taiwanese ingredients and flavors. Taiwanese cuisine is known for its bold flavors, creative use of ingredients, and street food culture. Some popular dishes in Taiwan include beef noodle soup, a hearty soup made with slow-cooked beef, noodles, and aromatic spices; stinky tofu, a pungent fermented tofu that is deep-fried and served with chili sauce; oyster omelet, a savory omelet made with fresh oysters, eggs, and vegetables; and xiao long bao, a type of steamed dumpling filled with juicy pork and soup.

Taiwan is also famous for its night markets, where visitors can sample a wide variety of street foods such as grilled squid, fried chicken, bubble tea, and shaved ice desserts.

Kids & Family

There are plenty of fun and exciting things that kids and families can do in Taiwan. Here are some ideas: Visit amusement parks: Taiwan has several amusement parks that offer a variety of rides and attractions suitable for all ages, such as Taipei Children's Amusement Park, Leofoo Village Theme Park, and E-DA Theme Park.

Explore museums: Taiwan has many museums with interactive exhibits and educational programs that are perfect for families, such as the National Museum of Natural Science in Taichung and the National Palace Museum in Taipei. Enjoy nature: Taiwan is home to many beautiful natural landscapes that are perfect for hiking, picnicking, and exploring, such as Taroko National Park and Yangmingshan National Park.

Attractions

Taiwan is a vibrant and diverse destination with a wide range of attractions to explore. Here are some popular attractions in Taiwan: Taipei 101: A landmark skyscraper and observation tower in Taipei, standing at 508 meters tall. Taroko National Park: A stunning natural wonderland of steep gorges, marble cliffs, and crystal-clear rivers.

Sun Moon Lake: A scenic lake in central Taiwan surrounded by mountains and lush greenery, offering a variety of outdoor activities such as hiking and cycling. Alishan National Scenic Area: A mountainous area in central Taiwan famous for its tea plantations, hiking trails, and scenic railway. Kenting National Park: A popular beach resort area in southern Taiwan, featuring white sand beaches, coral reefs, and a variety of water sports.

Shopping

Taiwan is a shopping paradise, offering a variety of shopping experiences ranging from high-end luxury malls to vibrant street markets. Here are some popular shopping destinations in Taiwan: Ximending: A lively pedestrian area in Taipei known for its trendy fashion boutiques, street food, and entertainment.

Shilin Night Market: One of the largest and most popular night markets in Taipei, offering a wide range of food, fashion, and souvenirs. Taipei 101: A world-class shopping destination located in Taipei's iconic skyscraper, featuring luxury boutiques, international brands, and gourmet dining. Guanghua Digital Plaza: A tech-lover's paradise located in Taipei, offering a huge selection of electronic gadgets and accessories.

Transportation

Taiwan has a well-developed transportation system that makes it easy and convenient to get around the country. Here are some of the main modes of transportation in Taiwan: MRT: The Mass Rapid Transit system, also known as the Taipei Metro, is a fast and efficient way to travel around Taipei and the surrounding areas. The MRT covers most of the city and is relatively inexpensive.

High-Speed Rail (HSR): The Taiwan High-Speed Rail system connects major cities such as Taipei, Taoyuan, Taichung, Tainan, and Kaohsiung. The HSR is clean, comfortable, and fast, making it an excellent option for longer distances. Bus: Taiwan has an extensive bus network that connects cities, towns, and villages across the island. Buses are generally affordable and convenient, with frequent schedules and routes.
